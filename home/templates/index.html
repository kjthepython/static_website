Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a common medical condition in which the force of blood against the walls of the arteries is consistently too high. High blood pressure is a major risk factor for a variety of serious health problems, including heart disease, stroke, and kidney disease.

Thyroid Treatment

Thyroid treatment depends on the specific thyroid disorder and the severity of the condition. The two most common thyroid disorders are hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid) and hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid).

Diabetic Neuropathy

Diabetic neuropathy is a type of nerve damage that occurs in people with diabetes. High blood sugar levels over time can damage the small blood vessels that supply nerves, leading to nerve damage. Diabetic neuropathy can affect any part of the body, but it most commonly affects the legs and feet.

Diabetes Kidney Failure Treatment

Diabetes is a leading cause of kidney failure, also known as diabetic nephropathy. Treatment for diabetes kidney failure typically involves a combination of medication, lifestyle changes, and possibly dialysis or kidney transplantation.

Diabetes Foot Specialist

A diabetes foot specialist, also known as a podiatrist, is a healthcare professional who specializes in the care and treatment of foot problems related to diabetes. People with diabetes are at increased risk of developing foot problems due to nerve damage and poor blood circulation, which can lead to foot ulcers and infections.
